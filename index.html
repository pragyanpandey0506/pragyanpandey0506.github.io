Projects

Quantum Phononic Simulator for Harper–Hofstadter–Hubbard Physics
2025 – Present
  • Initiated a collaboration with members of the Zaletel group (UC Berkeley) to design a chip-scale quantum electromechanical platform where high-Q nonlinear (Lennard–Jones) mechanical resonators realize interacting bosons, microwave resonators with phase-programmable drives generate a two-dimensional Harper–Hofstadter lattice with synthetic magnetic flux, and phononic crystals provide acoustic bandgaps and robust phonon confinement.
  • Derived the effective microwave-mediated electromechanical Hamiltonian and mean-field dynamics, and analyzed how drive phases and coupling strengths can be tuned to generate Chern bands and Harper–Hofstadter–Hubbard physics targeting fractional Chern insulator analogues of fractional quantum Hall states.
Inverse Design of Phononic Bandgap Shields Using Adjoint Optimization
2024 – 2025
  • Built finite-element band-structure models of silicon-based phononic bandgap shields and implemented a gradient-based optimization loop (finite-difference sensitivities with SLSQP) over lattice constant and shield dimensions to steer bandgaps toward target resonator frequencies, replacing trial-and-error geometry tuning.
  • Formulated an adjoint-based topology-optimization workflow that enables free optimization of the phononic-shield design space beyond tuning a small set of predefined geometric parameters.
Mechanical Qubit Based on Topologically Protected Helical Edge States in Snowflake Phononic Crystal
2024 – 2025
  • Proposed a mechanical qubit that encodes logical states in helical edge modes of a snowflake phononic crystal (Brendel et al., 2017), leveraging its established valley-Hall Dirac / valley-Chern description to obtain edge modes robust against backscattering.
  • Derived the electromechanical coupling Hamiltonian between these edge modes and superconducting transmon qubits in both dispersive and resonant Jaynes–Cummings regimes, and analyzed the resulting Kerr shifts and coherent swap operations.
  • Designed an optomechanical readout scheme where a co-localized optical cavity and pulsed homodyne detection distinguish clockwise versus counterclockwise edge excitations via mode-dependent sideband signals, and analyzed how weak DC acoustic perturbations can lift their degeneracy during measurement.
Century-Scale Map of Physics via Retroactive PhySH Annotation of the APS Corpus
2025 – Present
  • Mentored an undergraduate researcher and co-designed a pipeline using taxonomy-guided large language model prompting, strict schema validation, and canonical normalization to assign PhySH disciplines and up to five concepts to roughly 7.8 × 105 legacy APS articles (1893–2022), yielding a continuous century-scale subject map of physics.
  • Analyzed discipline and concept time series and cross-field couplings to recover canonical disciplinary arcs (nuclear and particle surges, condensed-matter dominance, persistent AMO), quantify rising interdisciplinarity, and distinguish long-lived methods (Monte Carlo, DFT, spectroscopy) from punctuated frontiers (superconductivity, quantum computing, two-dimensional materials).
