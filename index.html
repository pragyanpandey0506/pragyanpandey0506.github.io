Projects

Fractional Quantum Hall Effect in a Phononic Harper–Hofstadter Lattice
2025 – Present
  • Formulated a theoretical platform to realize fractional quantum Hall states in a two-dimensional phononic lattice implementing the Harper–Hofstadter model for mechanical modes.
  • Derived and analyzed a tight-binding description with synthetic magnetic flux and explored coupling strategies to microwave circuits and optical cavities for state preparation and readout.
Inverse Design of Phononic Bandgap Shields Using Adjoint Optimization
2024 – 2025
  • Built finite-element band-structure models of rectangular phononic bandgap shields in silicon frames and reproduced literature phononic bandgaps used to suppress clamping losses and enhance mechanical Q in membrane resonators.
  • Implemented a gradient-based optimization loop (finite-difference sensitivities with SLSQP) over lattice constant and cross dimensions to steer the phononic bandgap toward target resonator frequencies (e.g., 5.4 and 7 GHz), demonstrating that the shield geometry can be systematically tuned rather than selected by trial and error.
  • Designed an adjoint-based optimization workflow to maximize normalized bandgap width subject to fabrication constraints, with the goal of integrating optimized shields into microwave–optical transducers to increase Q·f and reduce radiation loss from the mechanical mode.
Mechanical Qubit Based on Topologically Protected Helical Edge States
in Snowflake Phononic Crystal
2024 – 2025
  • Analyzed a snowflake phononic crystal as a valley-Hall topological insulator by deriving an effective Dirac Hamiltonian with a symmetry-breaking mass term and computing valley Chern numbers that predict helical edge modes at domain walls.
  • Defined logical qubit states in terms of single-phonon occupation of counter-propagating edge modes and derived the electromechanical coupling Hamiltonian between those modes and superconducting transmon qubits in both dispersive and resonant Jaynes–Cummings regimes, including induced Kerr nonlinearities and swap operations.
  • Developed an optomechanical readout scheme in which a co-localized optical cavity discriminates clockwise and counterclockwise edge excitations via mode-dependent sideband signals under homodyne detection, and analyzed symmetry-breaking perturbations required to lift mode degeneracy during measurement.
Century-Scale Map of Physics via Retroactive PhySH Annotation of the APS Corpus
2025 – Present
  • Designed a large-scale pipeline that uses taxonomy-aware prompting of large language models, strict schema validation, and canonical normalization to assign PhySH disciplines and faceted concepts to ~7.8 × 105 APS papers from 1893–2022, producing a century-scale subject map of physics.
  • Constructed time series of discipline and concept frequencies and co-occurrence networks to recover canonical disciplinary arcs (e.g., nuclear and particle surges, condensed-matter dominance, persistent AMO), quantify rising interdisciplinarity, and study concept lifecycles ranging from long-lived methods (e.g., DFT, Monte Carlo) to punctuated frontier topics (e.g., superconductivity, quantum information, 2D materials).
